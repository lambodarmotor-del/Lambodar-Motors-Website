5 Signs Your Car's AC Needs Immediate Repair

Don't wait for a complete breakdown. Protect your car's cooling system from the extreme heat of Ahmedabad summers.

Driving in Ahmedabad during the peak summer months is almost impossible without a properly functioning car air conditioning system. When temperatures cross 40°C, your car's cabin can quickly heat up to dangerous levels if the AC fails. Knowing the early warning signs of system failure can help you get repairs done before the compressor fails completely.

1. Weak Airflow from the Vents

If you turn your AC blowers to maximum, but you only feel a gentle breeze, there is an issue. Weak airflow can be caused by a clogged cabin air filter, loose hoses, or a failing blower motor. A dirty cabin filter blocks dust and pollen but can also suffocate the system, forcing the compressor to work harder.

2. Blowing Warm or Hot Air

If your AC blowers are pushing warm or ambient air instead of cold air, it's a clear indicator of trouble. The most common reasons include a low refrigerant level (often due to a small leak in the hoses or condenser) or a malfunctioning AC compressor clutch. Continuing to run the system with low refrigerant can lead to compressor failure.

Important Tip: Running your AC with low refrigerant levels can permanently damage the compressor. Get a pressure check done immediately!

3. Strange Noises When Turning the AC On

It's normal to hear a faint click and a slight increase in engine RPM when the AC kicks in. However, if you hear loud grinding, squealing, or rattling noises, the compressor's internal parts or bearings may be worn out. These sounds mean immediate servicing is required before parts break apart inside the system.

4. Bad Smells Coming from the AC Vents

If you turn on your AC and get a damp, musty smell, mold or bacteria is likely growing inside your evaporator core. This is not only unpleasant but can trigger allergies and breathing issues. A professional car AC sanitize service clears this buildup and restores clean, healthy cabin air.

5. Water Puddles on the Passenger Side Floor

While you should see water dripping onto the road under your car while the AC runs (which is normal condensation drainage), water inside the cabin is a problem. A clogged condensate drain line causes water to overflow into the passenger footwell, which can damp your carpets and rust floor panels.

Summary

For minor checks like replacing the cabin filter, the fix is quick and inexpensive. If it's a refrigerant leak or compressor failure, specialized equipment is needed to capture and refill the gas safely.

How to Prepare Your Car for Monsoon in Gujarat | Gota, Ahmedabad

Prepare your car for Gujarat's heavy monsoons. Get Shailesh Bhai's essential monsoon checklist for Gota, Ahmedabad drivers.

Monsoons in Gujarat bring much-needed relief from the heat, but they also bring waterlogged roads in Gota, heavy downpours, and slippery highways. Driving in the rain requires your gaadi to be in top shape. Here is how you can prepare your car to stay safe this rainy season.

1. Worn Wipers and Windshield Care

Your wipers are your primary safety guard in heavy rain. During our harsh summers, the rubber on wiper blades dries up and cracks. When the rain starts, cracked wipers will smudge the glass, severely reducing visibility.

Replace your wiper blades if they leave streaks or make squeaking noises. Also, make sure to refill your windshield washer fluid with a dedicated wiper soap to clear away oil film and grime.

2. Tyre Tread and Braking Power

Slippery roads require excellent tire grip. If your tires are bald (tread depth below 2mm), your car is at high risk of hydroplaning—where the tires lose contact with the road and slide on a layer of water.

Check your tire tread and brake pads. Worn-out brake pads take longer to stop on wet surfaces. Shailesh Prajapati advises checking brakes before the first shower to ensure safety.

3. Electrical Inspections and Battery Health

Moisture is the enemy of car electronics. Cracked wiring insulation can cause short circuits in the rain, blowing fuses or leaving you stranded. Ensure all headlights, tail lights, fog lights, and indicators are working properly.

A weak battery is more likely to fail in damp weather. Get your battery voltage tested at our car workshop Gota to prevent starter failures during waterlogging.

Pro Tip from Shailesh Bhai: If you are stuck in waterlogged roads, keep your engine revving high in 1st or 2nd gear. This keeps exhaust pressure high and prevents water from entering the tailpipe!

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is hydroplaning?

Hydroplaning happens when a layer of water builds up between your car tires and the road surface, leading to a loss of traction and steering control.

Q: How often should wiper blades be replaced?

Wiper blades should be replaced once a year, ideally right before the monsoon season starts.

Q: What to do if water enters the engine?

NEVER try to restart the engine if it stalls in deep water. This can cause hydrostatic lock, destroying your engine piston rods completely. Call a tow truck immediately.

Q: How do I prevent my windows from fogging?

Turn on your AC, set it to fresh air mode, and direct the vents towards the windshield. This removes moisture from the cabin quickly.

Q: Should I apply anti-rust coating before monsoon?

Yes, underbody anti-rust coating protects the exposed metal of your chassis from salt and moisture, preventing severe rust damage.

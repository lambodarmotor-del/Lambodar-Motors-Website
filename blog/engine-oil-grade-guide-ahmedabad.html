How to Pick the Right Engine Oil Grade in Ahmedabad

Choosing the right engine oil grade for Gota, Ahmedabad's extreme climate? Learn from Shailesh Bhai Prajapati's guide.

Engine oil is the blood of your gaadi's engine. But with grades like 5W-30, 15W-40, mineral, and synthetic, picking the right oil can feel confusing, bhai! In a city like Ahmedabad where temperatures fluctuate from 10°C in winter to 48°C in summer, picking the wrong oil can damage your engine.

1. Understanding Oil Viscosity (The Numbers)

Viscosity is the thickness of the oil. In a grade like 5W-30, the 'W' stands for Winter. The number before 'W' (5) shows how easily the oil flows in cold weather. The second number (30) shows the oil's resistance to thinning at high engine operating temperatures.

In Ahmedabad's hot climate, you need an oil that doesn't thin out under extreme summer heat. Using an oil with a low high-temperature rating (like 0W-20 in a heavy SUV) can reduce engine lubrication protection.

2. Mineral, Semi-Synthetic, or Fully Synthetic?

Mineral oil is basic refined crude oil. Semi-synthetic is a blend, and fully synthetic is chemically engineered for high performance. Fully synthetic oil provides superior thermal stability, meaning it doesn't break down easily at 45°C ambient temperatures.

Synthetic oil costs more upfront, but it lasts longer (up to 10,000 km) and keeps your engine running cooler and cleaner. It is a highly recommended choice for modern hatchbacks and SUVs.

3. Checking Your Car Manual

Always refer to the manufacturer's manual. A Maruti Swift might require 0W-16 or 5W-30, while a diesel Scorpio might need 15W-40. Shailesh Prajapati warns against using heavy diesel oils in petrol cars, as this reduces performance and causes sluggishness.

Pro Tip from Shailesh Bhai: Check your engine oil level using the dipstick once a month. In hot climates, engines can naturally consume a tiny amount of oil, and running on low oil is extremely dangerous!

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I mix different brands of engine oil?

Yes, as long as the viscosity grade and oil type (synthetic/mineral) are the same, but it is always best to use the same brand for consistent performance.

Q: Why is my engine oil black?

Engine oil naturally turns black because it cleans carbon deposits and soot from the engine. It means the oil is doing its job, but it still needs replacement on time.

Q: How long does semi-synthetic oil last?

Semi-synthetic oil generally lasts about 7,500 km or 6 months, whichever comes first.

Q: What happens if I put too much oil?

Too much oil can cause foaming, which reduces oil pressure and prevents proper lubrication, potentially causing seal leaks.

Q: Why does my oil light turn on?

The oil light indicates dangerously low oil pressure. Stop the car immediately and check the dipstick level; driving with this light on can seize the engine.
